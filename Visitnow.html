Knossos

In a few words
Following the path and visiting Knossos palace, you travel to the depths of European history. The oldest civilization in Europe, the legend of King Minos and the Minotaur and the palace's artwork continues to inspire many generations of artists to this day. The palace was spectacular sophisticated and had sewer systems, luxurious, five-story houses and murals that adorned the walls in a unique way. It is said that its connection with the myth of the Minotaur also resembled a labyrinth. Eventually, it too was destroyed by an earthquake and abandoned forever around 1600 BC.
Mythology
Inside the palace, significant myths have been connected such as Minotaur Labyrinth and Daedalus and Icarus. According to mythology, Minos had been abducted from Zeus in form as a bull. Minos had the privilege to be king based on the law of palace but Zeus and the people of Crete they questioned this gods willingness. A bull had been sent from god Poseidon for sacrifice but Minos kept the bull as a highly valuable gift and instead of that sacrificed one of his own bull. As a punishment for unwillingness, Poseidon used an elixir and Minos wife Pasiphae fell in love with the bull, as a result, their love brought them to life the Minotaur, half-bull half-human form. Minos requested from great architect Daedalus to built-up a maze as Minotaurus prison. A vast of people had been sent to kill Minotaurus but ended up to be slaughtered by the bull.Every 9 years Zeus sent 7 rookies to sacrifice their lives until one rookie, Theseus fell in love with Minos daughter Ariadne and asked Daedalus to provide details related to the maze and how it was built-up. In that way Theseus got access and finally found the bull and terminated his life . As ultimate betrayal, Minos requested from the guards to exile Daedalus and his son Icarus in the maze but they built-up wax feathers to escape from the maze. Finally, they flew up to the sky but the sun burnt the wax and Icarus was killed.
Dont Miss
While you stand in the middle of the central yard, you will admire the majestic palace of Knossos, ready to reveal its secrets. Check at the east the royal residences as you walk among the houses with its murals and the famous dolphin mural that embraced the palace. Observe the details and be thrilled of Minoan daily life, feel the spirit and discover the history throught the ruins.
